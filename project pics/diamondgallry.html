MOST POPULAR TATTOO MEANINGS (WITH PICTURES)

Getting a tattoo is surely something exciting. However, if you want to get inked but you have no idea what to get, it can be overwhelming due to the countless options available. A good way to start figuring out what tattoo you want to get is by searching something that has meaning to you. Don't get us wrong, getting a tattoo with no meaning is completely okay! However, if you can find a tattoo you like that means something to you, it's is like a bonus. To help you find tattoo ideas with meaning, we've put together a list with some of the most popular tattoos, and a description of their different meanings. Keep in mind that tattoo meanings may vary from one culture to another - even from groups of people within the same culture -. Consequently, the ultimate list with all tattoo meanings doesn't exist. In this post, we've gathered the most relevant meanings in the western culture, and also in some eastern cultures. We hope the following list will help you find the meaning of that tattoo you've wanted for so long. Good luck!Visit 2nd page

    The folklore associated with bears is
    among the most ancient, dating back
    to Paleolithic times, from which there
    is surviving evidence of bear clans.
    BEAR TATTOO
    Bear tattoos and their most popular meanings:
    • In Native American cultures
      the bear is said to be
      the keeper of dreams,
      perhaps because the animal hibernates once a year.
    • The Viking had the bear as
      a symbol of strength and fury,
      and would
      often dress themselves in bear skins, eating
      hallucinogenic mushrooms to work themselves
      into a frenzy before entering into battle.
    • In many cultures, the bear
      is revered as a noble creature, with
      human-like treats.
    • The bear is also seen as a
      symbol for protection, due to the ferocity
      that bears display when
      protecting their cubs.
